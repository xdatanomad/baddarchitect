# Baddarchitect Article Style

Use this skill to write or revise AI Adoption Blueprint articles with the house voice: direct, candid, practical, human, evidence-aware, and concise. The goal is not pretty prose. The goal is useful thinking that serious AI adoption leaders trust enough to act on.

## Reference Map

- `references/style-principles.md`: house voice, cadence, formatting, credibility, evidence, and anti-patterns. Read before substantive drafting or rewriting.
- `references/section-patterns.md`: reusable structures for openings, signal sections, personal examples, checklists, operating models, and conclusions. Read when shaping article flow.
- `references/examples.md`: few-shot examples and before/after patterns. Read when the user asks for style matching, tone calibration, or a rewrite of existing prose.

## Required Context

Before drafting or revising an article, read:

1. `AGENTS.md`
2. `docs/vision.md`
3. The target article, draft, outline, or content-map entry

Then read only the source notes needed for the task from `docs/`, `content/notes/`, `content/ready/`, `research/`, or `website/`.

If the task involves a current factual claim, market data, vendor capability, benchmark, law, standard, or citation, verify it from a reliable source before presenting it as fact. Do not fabricate citations, studies, examples, metrics, or named customer stories.

## Workflow

1. Identify the reader/job, thesis, practical takeaway, and desired article type.
2. Choose the article structure before writing: problem/insight/action, signal/example/framework, or operating-model guide.
3. Draft or revise with rhythm: clear point, short narrative expansion, evidence or example, practical move, then a crisp turn.
4. Separate experience, opinion, and cited external claims.
5. Add a concrete reader action where useful: checklist, compact exercise, decision rule, operating compact, release gate, or architecture blueprint.
6. Tighten for reader attention: remove throat-clearing, hype, duplicate points, and generic transformation language.

## House Style Defaults

- Lead with the useful truth, not a slow setup.
- Use one-line paragraphs sparingly for emphasis, turns, and memorable conclusions.
- Prefer short-to-medium paragraphs with varied visual tempo.
- Use measured `we` or `in our experience` for credibility; use `I` only for real first-person observations.
- Use bold for important concepts, not decoration.
- Use italics for internal messages, quoted leadership language, or sharp contrasts.
- Use bullets for reader questions, checklists, contrasts, and operating rules.
- Make the first screen useful: metadata, reader/job, takeaway, and a clear opening tension.
- Keep the tone professional and humble. Be skeptical without becoming cynical.

## Relationship To Other Skills

This skill supplies the house article voice, cadence, and structure. It can be used alongside:

- `content-editor` for broader editorial challenge, publishability review, and thesis strengthening.
- `relatable-example` when the article only needs one inserted example.
- `actionable-takeaway` when the article only needs one practical reader action.

When the user asks for a full article draft or a style rewrite, this skill is enough. When the user asks for a formal review, use `content-editor` first, then apply this skill after the editorial direction is accepted.

## Quality Check

Before finishing, verify:

- The reader knows what to do differently after reading.
- The article respects the project's stance: AI adoption is a systems problem, not a tooling problem.
- The prose includes enough human context to feel credible, but not so much that it becomes memoir.
- Citations support factual claims instead of decorating opinions.
- The formatting has rhythm without becoming choppy.
- The final section lands on a practical conclusion, not a motivational slogan.
